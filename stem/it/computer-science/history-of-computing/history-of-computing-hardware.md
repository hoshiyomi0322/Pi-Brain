# First Generation (1951~1959)

    - #### type：electronic component

- ### Magnetic Drums
 
    - #### type：memory device
- ### Card Readers
 
    - #### type：punched card input device
    - #### IBM card
 
- ### Magnetic Tape
 
    - #### type：storage devices
        - sequential access
    - #### Magnetic Tape Drives
 
- ### Peripheral Devices

# Second Generation (1959~1965)
- ### Transistor
 
    - #### type：electronic component
    - #### pros：small, reliable, fast, durable, cheap
    - #### replaced Vacuum Tubes
- ### Magnetic Cores
 
    - #### type：memory device
        - random access
        - information available instantly
    - #### replaced Magnetic Drums
- ### Magnetic Disks
 
    - #### type：storage devices
        - random access
        - data can be accessed directly
    - #### replaced Magnetic Tape

# Third Generation (1965~1971)
- ### Integrated Circuits
 
    - #### type：electronic component
    - #### pros：small, reliable, fast, cheap
    - #### replaced Printed Circuit Boards
- ### Transistor
   
    - #### type：memory device
        - volatile memory
- ### Terminal
    - #### type：input/output device
        - with a keyboard and screen
- ### Moore's law
    - #### ver.1：the number of transistors in an integrated circuit doubles every year

# Fourth Generation (1971~)
- ### Very Large-Scale Integration
 
    - #### type：electronic component
- ### Personal Computers
    - #### Workstations
 
    - #### new companies emerge：Apple, Sun, Dell…
- ### Laptops, Tablet Computers, Smart Phones
- ### Moore's law
    - #### ver.2：the number of transistors in an integrated circuit doubles every 18 months
    - #### ver.3：the computer chip performance double every 18 months, or the price drops by half
